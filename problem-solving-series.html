Problem Solving Series

Mathematicians need to be good problem solvers. Textbooks and papers include sometimes include solutions to problems, but the entire endevor that the student must overcome is not normally explained. In this seminar series (held once per week, 15 sessions total), we will explore the different facets of problem solving.

1. Week 1: Always Move Forward

A common pet peeve of mine is whenever a person takes a class, then sometime later completely forgets how the subject operated. Our natural biology dictates that we must forget some information. There are pathological brains which do not forget anything, a cruel fate by which those aflicted are brought to tears being unable to forget exact recipt numbers.

What I encounter in college is that students will take some math class, get an A, then by the next semester they will completely forget the material and will proudly display how much they forgot.

What I want students to do is to build up a robust method by which they will always have access to the information they are taught. Perhaps those with strong memory could have this method of access be purely mental. What I am expecting reasonable students to do is to create a system of notes that is convinient to sift through and find the exact definitions we need.

The importance of having these notes is to build up a solid foundation in mathematics that always grows with further study. What I do not want people to suffer through is to learn a semseters worth of material only to forget the last semesters material (a zero sum game). I want people to go through this semester with the bonus knowledge at hand forevermore.
