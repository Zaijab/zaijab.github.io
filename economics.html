1.5.2. EQN

  1. Solow Growth Model

    y = f(k) y = c + i

    y - RGDP per worker c - consumption per worker i - investment per worker s - rate of savings

    i = sy c = (1 - s)y

    i = s f(k) - production function Depreciation = dk + nk + gk

    d - depreciation rate

    delta k = i - dk

    Investment flows into a tub, depreciation flows out of the tub. The amount of water in the tub is k, capital-labor ratio.

    n - labor growth rate

    Dilution = nk

1.5.3. Steady State

k = 0, capital-labor ratio does not change s f(k*) = dk*

2. Microeconomics

2.1. Supply Curve

Relationship between the quantity of a good that producers are willing to sell and the price of the good.

\(Q_s = Q_s(P)\)

It is a function of price, however price falls on the vertical axis lmao. It is upward sloping, there is a positive correlation between the price of a good and how much quantity a firm would produce.

Over variable can also affect supply, such as wages, interest, and cost of materials. When production costs decrease output increases no matter the output price.

2.2. Demand Curve

Relationship between the quantity of a good that consumers are willing to buy and the price of a good. \(Q_D = Q_D(P)\)
