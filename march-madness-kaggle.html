1.3. Feature Engineering

With all of the data at our disposal, let us decide on what shall be out inputs.

We know (as per the rules of the competition) that the outputs are

""" predicted probabilities for every possible matchup before the 2023 tournament begins. """

This entails we submit predictions for all 130,683 combinations of teams which can compete. However, """Most prediction rows will be discarded because one or both teams failed to make the tournament, or didn't play each other in the tournament.""".

Hence, we may split the data into teams which actually made it into the tournament and teams which did not make it into the tournament. The reason this is important is because teams which make it in, are given a "seed" which are predictions by a committee about who are the best teams in a given region. Teams which did not make it in will not have a seed, and will be discarded according to the competition rules. Hence, we will save ourselves computation time and avoid headaches of NaN values by moving these teams into a separate data frame with a baseline prediction of \( 0.5 \).
