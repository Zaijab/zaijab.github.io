Differential Equations

1. Violation of Existance and Uniqueness 1

Give an example of an ODE with both:

  • No solutions at a certain point
  • Infinitely many solutions at a certain point

1.1. Solution

\[ xy' = 1 - y \]

Can be solved,

\begin{alignat}{2} &&xy' &= 1 - y \tag{Given} \\ &\implies &\frac{1}{1 - y} dy &= \frac{1}{x} dx \tag{Separation of Variables} \\ &\implies & \int \frac{1}{1 - y} dy &= \int \frac{1}{x} dx \tag{Integrating} \\ &\implies & \ln(|1 - y|) &= \ln(|x|) + c \tag{Integrating} \\ &\implies & 1 - y &= cx \tag{Exponentiating} \\ &\implies & 1 - cx &= y \tag{Isolating $y$} \end{alignat}

Hence every solution passes through \( (0,1) \). There are infinitely many such solutions (one per value of \( c \)). There are no solutions passing through \( (0, 2) \).

3. Differential Equation with only local solution but no global solution

Give an example of a differential Equation with only local solution but no global solution.

3.1. Solution

\[ \dot{x} = x^2 \]

This may be solved by,

\begin{alignat}{2} && \dot{x} &= x^2 \\ &\implies & \frac{1}{x^2} dx &= dt \\ &\implies & \frac{-1}{x} &= t + c \\ &\implies & \frac{-1}{t + c} &= x \\ \end{alignat}

Note, given a choice of \( c \), \( x(t) \) will not be defined when \( t = -c \).

If \( x(0) = 4 \) then \( \frac{-1}{x(0)} = 0 + c \implies c = \frac{-1}{4} \) so the dynamical system is not defined for \( t = \frac{1}{4} \).
