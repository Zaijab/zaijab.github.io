6. The Plan

We may flowchart a solution technique which should work for every math problem in graduate school. 7.4. Average Value of Wave Equation

Statement:

Let \( \Omega = \left\{ x \in \mathbb{R}^{3} \middle| \left| x \right| < 1\right\} \) and let \( u(x,t) \) be a smooth solution of the wave equation

\begin{align} u_{tt} &= \Delta u &\quad & \text{in } \Omega \times (0,\infty) \\ \frac{ \partial u}{ \partial n} &= 0 &\quad & \text{on } \partial \Omega \times (0,\infty) \end{align}

For each \( r \in (0,1) \) and \( t \in (0,\infty) \), let \( U(r,t) \) denote the average value of \( u(\cdot,t) \) over a sphere of radius \( r \). Show that

\[ \int_{0}^{1} r^{2} U_{tt}(r,t) dr = 0 \]

for all \( t \).

Step 1: Make the problem amenable.

Let:

\( u(x,t) \) satisfy

\begin{align} u_{tt} &= \Delta u &\quad & \text{in } \Omega \times (0,\infty) \\ \frac{ \partial u}{ \partial n} &= 0 &\quad & \text{on } \partial \Omega \times (0,\infty) \end{align}

Denote

\[ U(r,t) = \frac{1}{\mu(\partial B(x,r))} \int_{\partial B(x, r)} u(y, t) dy \]

Recall that we are in \( \mathbb{R}^{3} \) so the sphere is a \( 2 \) dimensional object which is the boundary of the (3D) ball.

\begin{align} \int_{0}^{1} r^{2} U_{tt}(r,t) dr &= \int_{0}^{1} r^{2} \frac{ \partial^{2} }{ \partial t^{2}} \frac{1}{\mu(\partial B(x,r))} \int_{\partial B(x, r)} u(y, t) dy dr \\ &=\frac{ \partial^{2} }{ \partial t^{2}} \int_{0}^{1}\int_{\partial B(x, r)} r^{2} \frac{1}{\mu(\partial B(x,r))} u(y, t) dy dr \\ &=\frac{ \partial^{2} }{ \partial t^{2}} \int_{B(x, r)} r^{2} \frac{1}{\mu(\partial B(x,r))} u(y, t) dy dr \\ &= \int_{B(x, r)} r^{2} \frac{1}{\mu(\partial B(x,r))} u_{tt}(y, t) dy \\ &=r^{2} \frac{1}{\mu(\partial B(x,r))} \int_{B(x, r)} \Delta u(y, t) dy \\ &=r^{2} \frac{1}{\mu(\partial B(x,r))} \int_{B(x, r)} \Delta u(y, t) dy \\ &=r^{2} \frac{1}{\mu(\partial B(x,r))} \int_{B(x, r)} \Delta u(y, t) dy \\ \end{align}

10. Supremum of Convex Functions are Convex

Prove that the supremum of any collection of convex functions on \( (a,b) \) is convex on \( (a,b) \) (if it is finite) and that pointwise limits of sequences of convex functions are convex. What can you say about upper and lower limits of sequences of convex functions?

First things first, get the definitions straight and write out the problem statement.

Definitions:

Convex: \( \varphi \colon (a , b) \to \mathbb{R} \) is convex if the function values are lower than the line connecting two points: \[ \varphi((1 - t)x + t y) \leq (1 - t) \varphi(x) + t \varphi(y) \]

Supremum: Least Upper Bound

\[ s = \sup X \iff \forall x \in X \colon x \leq s \land (\forall L \in \mathbb{R} \colon x \leq L \implies s \leq L) \]

Problem Statement 1:

\begin{align*} & &&\forall \alpha \in A \colon \forall x, y \in (a,b) \colon \forall t \in [0,1] \colon \left( \varphi_{\alpha}((1 - t)x + ty) \leq (1 - t) \varphi_{\alpha}(x) + t \varphi_{\alpha}(y) \implies \sup_{\alpha \in A} \varphi_{\alpha}((1 - t)x + ty) \leq \sup_{a \in A} \left( (1 - t) \varphi_{\alpha}(x) + t \varphi_{\alpha}(y) \right) \right) \end{align*}

Direct Method: (Unravel Implication) Assume:

\[ \forall \alpha \in A \colon \forall x, y \in (a,b) \colon \forall t \in [0,1] \colon \varphi_{\alpha}((1 - t)x + ty) \leq (1 - t) \varphi_{\alpha}(x) + t \varphi_{\alpha}(y) \]

Let \( \alpha \in A \) be arbitrary.

\[ \forall x, y \in (a,b) \colon \forall t \in [0,1] \colon \varphi_{\alpha}((1 - t)x + ty) \leq (1 - t) \varphi_{\alpha}(x) + t \varphi_{\alpha}(y) \]

Let \( x, y \in (a,b) \) be arbitrary.

\[ \forall t \in [0,1] \colon \varphi_{\alpha}((1 - t)x + ty) \leq (1 - t) \varphi_{\alpha}(x) + t \varphi_{\alpha}(y) \]

Let \( t \in [0,1] \) be arbitrary.

\[ \varphi_{\alpha}((1 - t)x + ty) \leq (1 - t) \varphi_{\alpha}(x) + t \varphi_{\alpha}(y) \]

Now that we have properly "unraveled" everything. Now we do the fun part of implications. We need to show,

\[ \sup_{\alpha \in A} \varphi_{\alpha}((1 - t)x + ty) \leq \sup_{a \in A} \left( (1 - t) \varphi_{\alpha}(x) + t \varphi_{\alpha}(y) \right) \]

\begin{align*} \varphi_{\alpha}((1 - t)x + ty) &\leq \sup_{\alpha \in A} \varphi_{\alpha}((1 - t)x + ty) &&\text{Supremum is Upper Bound} \\ &\leq (1 - t) \varphi_{\alpha}(x) + t \varphi_{\alpha}(y) &&\text{Supremum is Least of Upper Bounds (Convex)} \\ &\leq \sup_{a \in A} \left( (1 - t) \varphi_{\alpha}(x) + t \varphi_{\alpha}(y) \right) &&\text{Supremum is Upper Bound} \end{align*}
