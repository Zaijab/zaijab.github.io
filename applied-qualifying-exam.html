Applied Qualifying Exam

1. Overview of the Subject

The applied qualifying exam at UH is designed for students to display mastery over the methods of mathematics that appear in the natural sciences. This part of math finds itself on the intersection of intuition, rigor, and results. That is, every problem will have its inspiration derived from some scientific question.

  • How will heat diffuse in a rod?
  • How will much charge is contained in a certain region of interest?
  • How does the sound change in a guitar as we increase the length of the string?
  • How can I model an outbreak of diesease or insects?

Solving these questions will have an important consequence in the world. We also believe in, "perfect is the enemy of good". If our analysis gets us most of the way to an answer, we can at least start implementing solutions based on the approximately correct response.

3.1.2. Anti-Diagonal Matrix   Problem_6_August_2017

  1. Problem

    Let \( A \in \mathbb{C}^{n \times n} \) be an anti diagonal matrix.

    \[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & a_{1} \\ 0 & 0 & \cdots & a_{2} & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 0 & a_{n-1} & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ a_{n} & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \]

    Under what conditions on \( a_{1}, \ldots, a_{n} \) is \( A \) unitarily diagonalizable.

  2. Solution

    By the spectral theorem, a complex matrix is unitarily diagonalizable iff it is normal. Hence, we compute the following products:

    \[ AA^{*} = \text{diag}(|a_{1}|^{2}, \ldots, |a_{n}|^{2}) \] \[ A^{*}A = \text{diag}(|a_{n}|^{2}, \ldots, |a_{1}|^{2}) \]

    Thus, \( A \) is unitarily diagonalizable over \( \mathbb{C} \) iff \( A \) is normal iff \( a_{1} = a_{n}, a_{2} = a_{n - 1}, \ldots, a_{i} = a_{n - i + 1} \).

3.1.4. \( \rank(A - B) = \rank(A) - 1 \iff B = \frac{Ax y^T A}{y^T A x} \)   Problem_6_August_2021

  1. Problem

    If \( A, B \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n} \) and \( \rank(B) = 1 \), then show that \( \rank(A - B) = \rank(A) - 1 \) iff \( B = \frac{Ax y^T A}{y^T A x} \)

  2. Solution

    Because \( \rank(B) = 1 \) we have \( B = vw^T \) for vectors \( v,w \).

    We also have the subadditivity of rank, which says \( \rank(A - B) \leq \rank(A) + \rank(B) \)

    \[ \implies \]

    Suppose we have the relation, \[ \rank(A - B) = \rank(A) - 1 \]

    \begin{align} & &\rank(A - B) &= \rank(A) - 1 \\ &\implies &\rank(A-B) &\leq \rank(A) \\ &\implies &\nullity(A-B) &\geq \nullity(A) \\ \end{align}

    Then we have two vectors

    \[ x \in \mathcal{N}(A - B) \setminus \mathcal{N}(A), y \in \mathcal{N}((A - B)^T) \setminus \mathcal{N}(A^T) \]

    Hence we have

    \begin{align} &&(A - B)x &= 0 \\ &\implies &Ax - Bx &= 0 \\ &\implies &Ax &= Bx \\ &\implies &0 \neq Ax &= Bx \tag{\( x \notin \mathcal{N}(A) \)} \end{align}

    Thus \( Ax = vw^Tx = (w^Tx) v \) which implies \( v = \frac{Ax}{w^Tx} \)

    We may do this similarly for \( y \), yielding an expression for \( w \).

    \begin{align} &&y^T(A - B) &= 0 \\ &\implies &y^{T}A - y^{T} B &= 0 \\ &\implies &y^{T}A &= y^{T}B \\ &\implies &0 \neq y^{T}A &= y^{T}B \tag{\( y \notin \mathcal{N}(A^{T}) \)} \end{align}

    Thus \( y^{T}A = y^{T}vw^T = (y^{T}v) w^{T} \) which implies \( w^{T} = \frac{y^{T}A}{y^{T}v} \)

    Thus \( y^{T}Ax = y^{T}vw^Tx = (y^{T}v) (w^{T}x) \neq 0 \).

    We may put this together,

    \[ B = v w^{T} = \left( \frac{Ax}{w^{T}x} \right) \left( \frac{y^{T}A}{y^{T}v} \right) = \frac{Axy^{T}A}{(w^{T}x)(y^{T} v)} = \frac{Axy^{T}A}{(y^{T} v) (w^{T}x)} =\frac{Axy^{T}A}{y^{T} A x} \]

    \[ \impliedby \]

    Suppose \( B = \frac{Ax y^T A}{y^T A x} \)

    There is strict containment \( \mathcal{N}(A) \subset \mathcal{N}(A - B) \)

    Suppose \( n \in \mathcal{N}(A) \)

    \begin{align} (A - B)n &= (A - \frac{Ax y^T A}{y^T A x})n \\ &= An - \frac{Ax y^T An}{y^T A x} \\ &= 0 - 0 \\ &= 0 \end{align}

    Note that \( Ax \neq 0 \) and \( x \in \mathcal{N}(A-B) \)

    \begin{align} (A - \frac{Ax y^T A}{y^T A x})x &= (Ax - \frac{Ax y^T A x}{y^T A x}) \\ &= (Ax - Ax) \\ &= 0 \\ \end{align}

    Because of the strict containment, we have a strict inequality of rank

    \[ \rank(A - B) < \rank(A) \]

    By subadditivity of rank, we have

    \[ \rank(A) = \rank(A - B + B) \leq \rank(A - B) + \rank(B) = \rank(A - B) + 1 \]

    We may put these two inequalities together,

    \[ \rank(A) - 1 \leq \rank(A - B) < rank(A) \]

    Hence \( \rank(A - B) = \rank(A) - 1 \)

3.1.5. \( A \) invertible integer matrix   Problem_6_January_2018

  1. Problem

    Let \( A \) be an invertible \( n \times n \) matrix with integer entries.

    Prove that if \( A^{-1} \) has integer entries iff \( | \det(A) | = 1 \)

  2. Solution

    Suppose that \( A^{-1} \) has integer entries Then \( \det(A^{-1}) \det(A) = \det(A^{-1} A) = \det(I) = 1 \) Because both \( A \) and \( A^{-1} \) have integer entries, their determinants are integers too. Hence the product of two integers, \( mn = 1 \) implies \( m, n = \pm 1 \).

    Suppose \( \left| \det(A) \right| = 1 \). Then \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(A)} \adj(A) \) We know that \( \det(A) = \pm 1 \) and \( \adj(A) \) has cofactors of \( A \) in each entry. This means that \( A^{-1} \) is the product of a scalar integer times a matrix with integers in each entry. This implies \( A^{-1} \) has integers in each entry.

3.1.6. Rank 1 Update Determinant Formula   Problem_6_January_2019

  1. Problem

    Suppose \( A, B \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n} \) and \( \rank(B) = 1 \). Show that the function \( f(t) = \det(A + tB) \) is a line.

  2. Solution

    Write \( B = vu^{T} \)

    By the rank 1 update formula

    \( \det(A + t v u^{T}) = \det(A + (tv)u^{T}) = \det(A) + (tv)^{T} \adj(A) u = \det(A) + (v^{T} A u) t \)

    Which is a line.

3.1.7. \( A = \frac{v v^{*}}{v^{*} v} \)   Problem_5_January_2020

  1. Problem

    Suppose \( 0 \neq v \in \mathbb{C}^{n} \) and \( A = \frac{vv^{*}}{v^{*}v} \)

    Part A
    What are the eigenvalues of A?
    Part B
    Is the matrix \( I + A \) diagonalizable?
    Part C
    Find \( \det(I + A) \)?
    Part D
    What is \( A^{2020} \)?
  2. Solution

    A has eigenvalues \( 1 \) with eigenvector \( v \). By calculation

    \[ Av = \frac{v v^{*} v}{v^{*}v} = v \]

    The orthogonal complement of the vector \( v^{\perp} \) has a basis of \( n - 1 \) vectors perpendicular to \( v \) and have eigenvalue \( 0 \).

    \[ A w = \frac{v v^{*}}{v^{*}v} w = 0 w \] Thus \( A \) has eigenvalues \( 1 \) with multiplicity \( 1 \) and \( 0 \) with multiplicity \( 0 \). The algebraic and geometric multiplicities are the same hence \( A \) is diagonalizable.

    If \( A = P^{-1} D P \) then \( I + A = P^{-1} P + P^{-1} D P = P^{-1}(I + D)P \)

    \( I + A \) is diagonalizable because \( A \) is diagonalizable.

    The determinant is the product of the eigenvalues. Using part A this says this is the product of \( 2^{1} \cdot 1^{n - 1} = 2 \). Another way to find this is to use the matrix determinant lemma.

    \( \det(I + A) = 1 + \frac{v^{*}v}{v^{*}v} = 2 \)

    By inspection, notice that \( A^{2} = \frac{vv^{*}}{v^{*}v} \frac{vv^{*}}{v^{*}v} = \frac{vv^{*}}{v^{*}v} = A \) hence \( A^{2020} = A \)

3.1.8. Show \( RR^{*} + S^{*} S \) is invertible   Problem_6_January_2022

  1. Problem

    Suppose we have the exact sequence

    \[ \xymatrix{ \mathbb{C}^n \ar[r]^{R} & \mathbb{C}^m \ar[r]^{S} & \mathbb{C}^k } \]

    Show that \( T = RR^{*} + S^{*} S \) is an invertible linear transformation.

  2. Solution

    Given,

    \[ \xymatrix{ \mathbb{C}^n \ar[r]^{R} & \mathbb{C}^m \ar[r]^{S} & \mathbb{C}^k } \]

    We have the following exact sequence,

    \[ \xymatrix{ \mathbb{C}^n & \ar[l]^{R^{*}} \mathbb{C}^m & \ar[l]^{S^{*}} \mathbb{C}^k } \]

    We may decompose \( \mathbb{C}^m \) into a direct sum due to it being a Hilbert Space.

    \begin{align} \mathbb{C}^m &= \mathcal{N}(S) \oplus \mathcal{N}(S)^{\perp} \tag{\(\( \mathcal{N}(S) \) is a closed subspace of \( \mathbb{C}^m \)\)} \\ &= \mathcal{N}(S) \oplus \mathcal{R}(S^{*}) \tag{Cheney Chapter 2 Theorem 7} \\ &= \mathcal{R}(R) \oplus \mathcal{N}(R^{*}) \tag{Exactness 1} \\ &= \mathcal{N}(S) \oplus \mathcal{R}(S^{*}) \tag{Exactness 2} \end{align}

    The last two lines of above are equally important. We will use this form to show that the transformations \( RR^{*} \) and \( S^{*}S \) are zero on one factor, and an isomorphism on the other. Hence, their sum is an isomorphism on the entire space.

    Note that \( RR^{*} \colon \mathbb{C}^m \to \mathcal{R}(R) \),

    \begin{align} RR^{*}v &= RR^{*}(v_{\mathcal{R}(R)} + v_{\mathcal{N}(R^{*})}) \tag{Exactness 1} \\ &= RR^{*}(v_{\mathcal{R}(R)}) \tag{Linearity of \( R^{*} \)} \\ &\in \mathcal{R}(R) \tag{Def. Image} \end{align}

    Then, as a map, \( RR^{*} \colon \mathcal{R}(R) \to \mathcal{R}(R) \), \( RR^{*} \) is injective.

    This is because

    1. \( \mathcal{N}(RR^{*}) = \mathcal{N}(R^{*}) \)

      By containment, \( \mathcal{N}(RR^{*}) \supseteq \mathcal{N}(R^{*}) \) is trivial. Suppose \( v \in \mathcal{N}(RR^{*}) \), then:

    \begin{align} 0 &= \langle RR^{*}v_{\mathcal{R}(R)} ,v_{\mathcal{R}(R)} \rangle \tag{Linearity of IP} \\ &= \langle R^{*}v_{\mathcal{R}(R)} , R^{*} v_{\mathcal{R}(R)} \rangle \tag{Def. Adjoint} \\ &= || R^{*}v_{\mathcal{R}(R)} ||^2 \tag{Def. Norm} \end{align}

    Hence \( R^{*}v = 0 \) by positive definiteness of norm.

    1. By Exactness 1 we have that \( \mathcal{R}(R) \cap \mathcal{N}(R^{*}) = \{ 0 \} \)

      Hence the null space of the endomorphism on \( \mathcal{R}(R) \) is trivial. Thus, \( R R^{*} \) is injective on \( \mathcal{R}(R) \). By Rank Nulity Theorem we have that this map is also surjective. Hence the map is an isomorphism.

    We may proceed similarly for the isomorphism of \( S^{*} S \).

3.3.3. Human Population Density   Problem_5_August_2017

  1. Problem

    Solve the PDE:

    \[ P_{t} + P_{y} + D(t,y) P = 0 \]

    With boundary condition \( P(t,0) = B(t) \) where \( B(t) \) and \( D(t,y) \) respectively represent the birth and death rates.

    1. Part A

      Using the method of characteristics, reduce the PDE to an ODE by making a suitable change of variables, and then find the general solution

  2. Solution
    1. Part A

      Let

      \begin{align*} \begin{cases} \hat{t} = t - y \\ \hat{y} = y \end{cases} \end{align*}

      Then the PDE transforms via the chain rule to:

      \begin{align} -D(t,y)P &= \frac{ \partial P}{ \partial t} + \frac{ \partial P}{ \partial y} \tag{PDE} \\ &= \frac{ \partial P}{ \partial \hat{t}} \frac{ \partial \hat{t}}{ \partial t} + \frac{ \partial P}{ \partial \hat{t}} \frac{ \partial \hat{t}}{ \partial y} + \frac{ \partial P}{ \partial \hat{y}} \frac{ \partial \hat{y}}{ \partial y} \tag{Chain Rule} \\ &= \frac{ \partial P}{ \partial \hat{t}} - \frac{ \partial P}{ \partial \hat{t}} + \frac{ \partial P}{ \partial \hat{y}} \tag{Change of Variables Differentials} \\ &= \frac{ \partial P}{ \partial \hat{y}} \tag{Simplify} \end{align}

      We are therefore given (effectively) a separable ODE in \( \hat{y} \). The Solution is then,

      \[ P(\hat{t},\hat{y}) = A(\hat{t}) \exp\left(\int_{0}^{\hat{y}} -D(\hat{t},\tilde{y}) d\tilde{y}\right) \]

      \[ P(t,y) = A(t - y) \exp\left(\int_{0}^{y} -D(t - y,\tilde{y}) d\tilde{y}\right) \]

      \[ P(t,0) = B(t) = A(t - y) \exp\left(\int_{0}^{0} -D(\hat{t},\tilde{y}) d\tilde{y}\right) = A(t - y) \]

6.9.4. Duhamel's Principle

Duhamel's Principle states:

To solve the equation

\[ u_{t} + L[u] = F(x,t) \]

Imagine solving the equation at time \( s > 0 \) with the initial condition given by the inhomogeneous component of the equation. Then adding all of the solutions for every \( s \) (continuously many, hence integrate from \( 0 \) to some \( t \)).

Concretely,

\begin{align} u_{t} - u_{xx} = F(x,t) \tag{PDE} \\ u(0,t) = 0 \tag{BC 1} \\ u(L,t) = 0 \tag{BC 2} \\ u(x, 0) = 0 \tag{IC} \end{align}

Then its solution is given by

\[ u(x,t) = \int_{0}^{t} v(x,\xi;s) d \xi \]

Where \( v \) solves the shifted heat equation

\begin{align} v_{t} - v_{xx} &= 0 \tag{PDE} \\ v(0,t) &= 0 \tag{BC 1} \\ v(L,t) &= 0 \tag{BC 2} \\ v(x, 0) &= F(x,s) \tag{IC} \end{align}

This is a homogeneous heat equation on a rod with homogeneous Dirichlet BC and a given initial condition. This has solutions given by Fourier series.

\[ v(x,t) = \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} b_{n} e^{-\lambda_{n}^{2}t} \sin(\lambda_{n}x) \, t \geq s \]

Remember this is a sin series because of the homogeneous Dirichlet BC.
