Noun Sentences "Sakura is Japanese." 桜が日本人だ。 だ

4. Connecting Clauses

Here we introduce another beautiful aspect of Japanese, conjunctions.

A conjunction is a "mashing" or "combining" of two different ideas into one part.

First, let us see this in English:

  1. We have a number of ideas.
    1. The girl sings.
    2. The girl is smart.
    3. The girl dances.
  2. We want to combine these ideas into one sentence.
    1. The smart girl who sang dances.
    2. The girl who danced and sang is smart.

Many sentences we speak are complex and do not often fall into the simple "I like bread" kind of sentences. In Japanese, the process of turning multiple sentences into one is very nice. The pattern is thus,

SENTENCE 1. SENTENCE 2. => [TE FORM OF "SENTENCE 1"] SENTENCE 2.
